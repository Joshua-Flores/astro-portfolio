## Why Claude?

As someone who's been building frontends for a while now, I'm always looking for ways to move faster without sacrificing quality. I started using Claude a few months ago and it's become a regular part of how I work. It's not about replacing what I know — it's about having a second brain that can keep up with the pace of modern frontend development.

## Where It Shines

### Prototyping Components

One of the biggest time sinks in frontend work is going from a design to a working component. Claude is excellent at taking a description of what I need and spitting out a solid first draft. I'll describe the component, mention the framework (React, Astro, whatever I'm working in), and get back something I can immediately start iterating on.

For example, I recently needed a responsive card grid with hover effects and skeleton loading states. Instead of writing it from scratch, I described what I wanted and had a working version in under a minute. From there it was just tweaking to match the design system.

### Debugging CSS

We've all been there — something isn't aligning right, a flexbox layout is doing something unexpected, or a z-index issue is driving you insane. I've found that pasting the relevant markup and styles into Claude and describing the issue usually gets me to the fix faster than digging through DevTools alone.

### Writing Tests

This is the one that surprised me the most. I tend to procrastinate on writing tests, but Claude makes it almost effortless. I hand it a component and ask for test cases, and it comes back with comprehensive coverage including edge cases I might not have thought of.

## Where I Still Take the Wheel

Claude isn't perfect for everything. I still prefer to handle:

- **Architecture decisions** — Claude can suggest patterns, but I know my codebase and team conventions better
- **Performance optimization** — profiling and fixing bottlenecks still requires hands-on investigation
- **Design system consistency** — AI doesn't have the visual context of the full product

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of It

1. **Be specific about your stack** — mentioning Tailwind vs. vanilla CSS, React vs. Astro, etc. makes a huge difference in output quality
2. **Iterate, don't expect perfection** — treat the first response as a draft
3. **Use it for the boring stuff** — boilerplate, repetitive patterns, and documentation are where it saves the most time

## Final Thoughts

Claude hasn't changed what I build, but it's changed how fast I can build it. If you're a frontend developer who hasn't tried incorporating AI into your workflow yet, I'd recommend starting with the low-hanging fruit — component scaffolding and test writing — and going from there.
